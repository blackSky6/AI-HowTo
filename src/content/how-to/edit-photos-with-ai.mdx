## TL;DR

For quick edits on your phone, **Snapseed** with AI suggestions is free and excellent. For desktop power editing, **Adobe Lightroom's AI tools** (Denoise, Masking, Remove) are industry-leading. For e-commerce product photos, **Photoroom** is a game changer.

## Top Pick: Adobe Lightroom AI

Lightroom's AI features have matured into the best all-around photo editing solution. Select Subject, Denoise, and Generative Remove are genuinely magic.

- **Best for:** Photographers, content creators
- **Price:** $10/mo (Photography plan)
- **Why we love it:** One-click AI fixes that used to take hours

## The 5 Best AI Photo Editing Tasks

### 1. Remove Unwanted Objects
**Tool:** Adobe Lightroom (Generative Remove) or Canva (Magic Eraser)

Steps:
1. Open your photo in Lightroom
2. Go to the Healing tool → Select "Generative AI"
3. Paint over the object you want to remove
4. Click "Apply" — Lightroom fills it in seamlessly

### 2. Enhance and Denoise
**Tool:** Lightroom AI Denoise or Topaz Photo AI

1. Open photo → Develop module
2. Click "Denoise" button (top of Detail panel)
3. Set amount to 50-80 for most photos
4. Apply — removes grain while preserving detail

### 3. Replace Backgrounds
**Tool:** Photoroom or Canva AI

1. Upload your photo
2. Background is auto-removed
3. Choose a new background from their library or upload your own
4. Download

### 4. One-Click Enhance
**Tool:** Canva AI "Magic Enhance"

1. Upload to Canva → Edit Photo
2. Click "Enhance" → "Auto Enhance"
3. Adjusts brightness, contrast, color automatically

### 5. Upscale Low-Resolution Photos
**Tool:** Topaz Gigapixel or Adobe Super Resolution

1. Open a low-res photo
2. Click "Enhance" → "Super Resolution"
3. AI adds detail — doubles or quadruples resolution

## FAQ

**What's the best free AI photo editor?**
Canva's free tier covers most basic needs. Snapseed (mobile, free) is excellent for phone photos.

**Can AI fix blurry photos?**
AI can improve soft focus and noise, but can't fix a severely blurry or out-of-focus shot. Tools like Topaz Sharpen AI work best on slightly soft images.

**Is AI editing cheating in photography?**
It's a personal choice. Professional photographers use AI tools routinely — the goal is the final image, not the process.
